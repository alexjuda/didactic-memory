Setting up alpine for an Office email

Posted by flax on Sun 31 July 2022

Email TUIs

Each one comes with its own keybindings, and docs. However, it's not that easy to configure them to work with Office 365 or Microsoft Exchange account. The whole process is quite cumbersome. I suggest to start with the simplest client first â€“ alpine.

Gotchas

There's a nice page at University of Illinois that describes how to configure alpine for Office email. I've been able to apply the configuration to at least read the inbound emails.

Sending emails didn't work out of the box. Hopefully, there's a Google Groups thread about the dreaded:

Mail not sent. Sending error: 250

Also, when things go wrong, you might wanna make sure your sender name and sender email address match between the config file, the email header, and the email body. Otherwise, sometimes you get weird errors.

Config

Anyway, this is the .pinerc what I've ended up with.

personal-name=Firstname Lastname
user-domain=the.part.of.my.address.after.the.at.sign.com

smtp-server=smtp.office365.com:587/tls/novalidate-cert/user=my.email@address.com
inbox-path={outlook.office365.com:993/ssl/user=my.email@address.com}Inbox
default-fcc={outlook.office365.com:993/ssl/user=my.email@address.com}Sent Items
default-saved-msg-folder={outlook.office365.com:993/ssl/user=my.email@address.com}Archive
trash-folder={outlook.office365.com:993/ssl/user=my.email@address.com}mail/Trash

literal-signature=Firstname Lastname\n

feature-list=quell-user-lookup-in-passwd-file,
    suppress-asterisks-in-password-prompt

customized-hdrs=From: First Name <my.email@address.com>

Alpine asks for password on every app start. I haven't been able to integrate it with password managers like pass but hey. It works. I wanted to start simple.
